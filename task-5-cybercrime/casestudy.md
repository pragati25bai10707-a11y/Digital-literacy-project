# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive information. In a typical scenario, a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or fake link, pretending to be a bank official or a trusted service provider.

The fraud usually begins when the attacker sends a fake payment request or a malicious link. The victim is asked to click the link or approve a request, often under the pretext of receiving money or verifying their account. Once the victim enters their details or approves the transaction, money is deducted from their bank account.

Students and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment security are common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes misuse of personal data.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of being cautious while making online transactions and verifying any suspicious requests before taking action.
